What Did I Learn ?


👉 Declarative Sentences: These sentences state facts or opinions and end with a full stop (.).

Examples:

  • "The Earth revolves around the sun."
  • "My favourite book is 'Charlie and the Chocolate Factory'.
  • "Dogs make loyal pets."
Use declarative sentences when you want to share information or express your thoughts clearly.

👉 Imperative Sentences: These sentences give commands or make requests. They often start with a verb and can end with a period (.) or an exclamation mark (!).

Examples:

  • "Please turn off the lights when you leave the room." 💡
  • "Don't forget to bring your lunch to school." 🍱
  • "Finish your homework before watching TV." 📚
Use imperative sentences when you want to tell someone to do something or give instructions.

👉 Exclamatory Sentences: These sentences express strong emotions or sudden feelings and end with an exclamation mark (!).

Examples:

  • "What a fantastic performance that was!" 🎭
  • "I can't believe we won the championship!" 🏆
  • "Watch out for that puddle!" 💦
Use exclamatory sentences when you want to show excitement, surprise, anger, or any strong emotion.

👉 Interrogative Sentences: These sentences ask questions and end with a question mark (?).

Examples:

  • "What time does the movie start?" 🎥
  • "How do plants make their food?" 🌱
  • "Did you remember to feed the cat?" 🐱
Use interrogative sentences when you want to ask for information or clarification.


Why Understanding Sentence Types is Important:

  • Improved Writing: Knowing these sentence types helps you vary your writing style, making your essays, stories, and reports more interesting to read. ✍️
  • Better Communication: Using different sentence types allows you to express yourself more clearly and effectively in both speaking and writing. 🗣️
  • Reading Comprehension: Recognising sentence types can help you better understand what you're reading, especially the author's tone and intent. 📖

Words that I learnt from the story

Attic: A space or room just below the roof of a house, often used for storage.
Sentence: We found an old chest of books in the dusty attic of our grandparents' house. 📚

Hurriedly: In a quick or rushed manner, because of a lack of time.
Sentence: She hurriedly packed her bag when she realized she was late for school. 🏃‍♀️

Creeping: Moving slowly and quietly, often in a sneaky or cautious way.
Sentence: The cat was creeping through the tall grass, trying not to be seen by the birds. 🐈
